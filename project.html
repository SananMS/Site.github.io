Robotics

What is Robotics?

As technology progresses, so too does the scope of what is considered robotics. Robotics is the intersection of science, engineering and technology that produces machines, called robots, that substitute for (or replicate) human actions. In 2005, 90% of all robots could be found assembling cars in automotive factories. These robots consist mainly of mechanical arms tasked with welding or screwing on certain parts of a car. Today, we’re seeing an evolved and expanded definition of robotics that includes the development, creation and use of bots that explore Earth’s harshest conditions, robots that assist law-enforcement and even robots that assist in almost every facet of healthcare.
